Content Design for a Logistics App Targeting 20 to 45-Year-Old Entrepreneurial Women

The challenge is to design the app's content in a way that is relevant, engaging, and easy to use.

Women aged 20 to 45 represent a promising market segment for Skydropx. These businesswomen face specific challenges in managing their logistics operations. To address this need, Skydropx is interested in developing a mobile application that would allow them easy access to the company's services and provide tools for efficient logistics management.

The key findings in our research include

80% of surveyed women use their mobile phones as the primary tool for managing their businesses.
Common logistics management challenges like shipment tracking, inventory organization, payment methods, and customer support.
Preference for visual tutorials that helps them to learn quickly.

Some key strategies and considerations include

A simple and friendly wording that matches the tone they use in their everyday sales and customer attention.
Visuals and consistent terminology for the shipment tracking feature.
Clear and direct communication to ease user and Skydropx's customer support team interactions.

Expected results

The user-centric content design will improve their experience increasing user engagement, satisfaction, and loyalty towards the company.
Growth in the female customer base.
Gain recognition of the company as a brand committed to the needs of entrepreneurial women.
